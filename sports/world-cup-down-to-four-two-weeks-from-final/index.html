World Cup Down To Four, Which Is To Say Two Weeks From A Final That Starts In Six Days

The 2026 FIFA World Cup has reached its semifinals: Spain, France, England and Argentina. The final is scheduled for July 19. Social media, as ever, has already decided the outcome.

By The 2weeker Administration July 14, 2026

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is down to four teams. Spain, France, England and Argentina remain in a tournament that began June 11 across the United States, Mexico and Canada and is scheduled to end with the final on July 19. On the calendar, that is six days away. In the cosmology of this publication, it is two weeks from being decided.

(Update: Spain have since beaten France 2-0 in the first semifinal to reach the final. See the separate dispatch.)

The semifinals are set: England against Argentina, and Spain against France. Both are heavyweight pairings that, between them, contain the two players tied for the tournament’s golden boot — Lionel Messi and Kylian Mbappé, eight goals each, per Wikipedia’s tournament summary.

How We Got Here

France reached the final four by beating Morocco 2-0 in the quarterfinal on July 9, with Kylian Mbappé and Ousmane Dembélé scoring in Boston. Spain then eliminated France — a 21-year-old Lamine Yamal, whose birthday fell on the day of the match, at the center of a win that sent Spain to its second World Cup final, per FIFA’s own match reporting.

England’s path has been, by consensus, grim to watch and impossible to argue with. They beat Norway 2-1 after extra time in the heat of Miami on Saturday, July 11. Manager Thomas Tuchel, asked about his team’s mentality by ITV Sport, said: “This is pure mentality. You can bottle it up and sell it.” England full-back Djed Spence, who came off the bench to help drag his side through, told Pro Soccer Talk: “We are a family, we are altogether and we will die for each other.” Goalkeeper Jordan Pickford described the approach more bluntly: “You’ve sometime got to get in the trenches.”

Argentina, the defending champion, reached the last four by beating Switzerland 3-1 after extra time. Which means the other semifinal, England against Argentina in Atlanta on Wednesday, pairs the tournament’s two scrappiest survivors — and, as several outlets noted, will be Messi’s first competitive meeting with England’s senior side in his career.

What Twitter Is Saying

Direct scraping of X was unavailable for this dispatch, but reaction surfaced through the outlets that syndicate it. Telemundo Deportes (@TelemundoSports) posted on July 12 that England were hailing “incredible World Cup resilience” ahead of the Argentina clash. The BBC captured Argentine fans in the street expressing confidence about facing England, with one framing the matchup as a contest Argentina intend to win. On Instagram, fan accounts framed England-Argentina as “one game from immortality,” and Spanish-language posts described keeping Messi quiet as the central tactical problem for Wednesday.

The through-line of the online reaction is less about tactics than about narrative: two teams that have “found a way to win, somehow” (Yahoo’s phrasing on Argentina), meeting in a fixture loaded with the history both countries carry. The posts are, in the manner of World Cup social media, certain of the outcome and divided on which certainty is correct.

The Standing Forecast

Four teams, two matches, one final on July 19. The data says the title will be settled in six days. The form says England and Argentina will each insist they were the tougher team no matter what happens. And this publication says the whole thing is, as always, two weeks from being over — the trophy presentation simply has not received the memo.
